What is Pressure?

Ideally, why does a sharp thumbtack pierce cork easily, but the flat end doesn't?

Definition

Pressure is defined as the force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area over which that force is distributed.

P = F / A

P = Pressure | F = Force | A = Area

Think: How much push (force) is spread over how much space (area).

Pressure Calculation Triangles

Find Pressure (P)

F / A

Pressure = Force ÷ Area

Find Force (F)

P × A

Force = Pressure × Area

Find Area (A)

F / P

Area = Force ÷ Pressure

What is Density?

Which is heavier: a bag of feathers or a small rock? The rock is denser.

Definition

Density is a measure of how much mass is contained in a given volume.

ρ = m / V

ρ (rho) = Density | m = Mass | V = Volume

Common Units: kg/m³ (SI) or lbm/ft³ (Imperial)

Types of Pressure

Atmospheric

Weight of the air above us.
~14.7 psi / 101.3 kPa at sea level.

Gauge

Relative to atmospheric pressure. Reads 0 at sea level.

Absolute

Relative to a perfect vacuum (True Zero).

P_abs = P_gauge + P_atm

Vacuum

Pressure below atmospheric level.

[Image of Pressure Chart]

Differential Pressure (ΔP)

The difference between two pressures measured at different points.

Key Applications

  • Flow Rate: Across an orifice plate.
  • Level: In pressurized tanks.
  • Filters: High ΔP indicates a clogged filter.

Pressure in Fluids

Pressure increases with depth due to the weight of the fluid.

P = ρgh
ρ (Rho)

Density (kg/m³)

g

Gravity (9.81 m/s²)

h

Height/Depth (m)

Example: 5m deep water tank (1000 kg/m³)
P = 1000 × 9.81 × 5 = 49,050 Pa (49.05 kPa)

Calculating Force

We can rearrange P = F/A to find Force:

F = P × A

Example: Hydraulic Cylinder

  • Pressure: 1000 psi
  • Piston Area: 20 in²
  • Force = 1000 × 20 = 20,000 lbf
[Image of Hydraulic Force]

Pascal's Principle

"Pressure applied to a confined fluid is transmitted undiminished to every part of the fluid."

This is the heart of Hydraulics.

F1 / A1 = F2 / A2

A small force on a small area creates pressure that results in a LARGE force on a large area.

Hazards & Safety

Stored Energy

High pressure fluids contain massive energy. Ruptures can cause explosions and projectiles.

Injection Injuries

High-velocity leaks (pinholes) can penetrate skin causing severe damage.

Safety Measures

  • Relief Valves: Prevent over-pressure.
  • LOTO: Lock Out Tag Out before maintenance.
  • Purging: Depressurize lines fully.
  • PPE: Eye protection and gloves.
