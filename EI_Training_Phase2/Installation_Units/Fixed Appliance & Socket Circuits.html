Key Definitions

Portable Equipment

Equipment designed to be moved while in operation or easily moved while connected to the supply.

Hand-Held Equipment

Portable equipment intended to be held in the hand during normal use (motor is integral).

Stationary Equipment

Fixed equipment or equipment without a handle having a mass greater than 18kg (e.g., Washing Machine).

Fixed Equipment

Equipment fastened to a support or secured at a specific location.

Socket Outlet Types

13A Flat Pin (IS 411 / BS 1363)

The standard for domestic use. Features shuttered apertures for safety.

16A Side Earth (Continental)

Two pin with side earth contacts. Rare in modern Irish installations but found in guest houses.

5A Round Pin

Permitted by ETCI for connection of table lamps controlled by a central switch.

The 13A Plug Top

Safety Features

  • Fused: Protects the flexible cord (not the appliance).
  • Insulated Pins: Live and Neutral pins are insulated at the base to prevent shock if touched while partially inserted.
  • Shutter Interlock: Earth pin is longer to open the shutters on the socket.

Mounting Heights & Locations

Standard Height

450mm to centre of socket.

Range: 400mm - 1200mm above finished floor level (FFL).

Accessibility

For elderly or disabled access, increase height to 1100mm.

Kitchen Worktops

Must be at least 100mm above worktop level.

RCD Protection

All general-purpose socket outlets must be protected by a 30mA RCD.

Radial Final Circuit

A linear chain where each socket is fed from the previous one, terminating at the last socket.

Radial Rules

  • Max Area: 2 Rooms.
  • Max Sockets: 10 outlets.
  • Cable: Min 2.5mm².
  • Protection: 20A MCB (Type B).
Note: A kitchen should be supplied by at least two radial circuits.

Ring Final Circuit

A loop starting and ending at the same terminals in the distribution board. Saves copper and allows higher loads.

Ring Rules

  • Max Area: 100m² floor area.
  • Sockets: Unlimited (within area).
  • Cable: 2.5mm².
  • Protection: 32A MCB.
CCC: Cable Current Carrying Capacity must be ≥ 0.67 times the rating of the protective device. (32 x 0.67 = 21.44A).

Spurs (Branch Connections)

A spur is a branch connection from a ring final circuit.

Unfused Spur

Connected directly to the ring cable.

Limit: Only one socket outlet (single or twin) allowed per unfused spur.

Fused Spur

Connected via a Fused Connection Unit (FCU).

Can supply fixed appliances or multiple sockets (as the fuse limits the current).

Testing: Polarity

WARNING

This test must be carried out with the supply DISCONNECTED.

Purpose

To ensure:

  • Phase (L) is connected to the fuse/switch.
  • Neutral (N) is connected to the neutral terminal.
  • Earth (E) is connected to the earth terminal.
  • ES lamp centre contact is Live.

Method (Dead Test)

  1. Remove circuit fuse/open MCB.
  2. Unplug all appliances.
  3. Connect a long trailing lead to the Phase at the distribution board.
  4. Test to Phase terminal at every socket using an Ohmmeter.
  5. Reading should be very low (close to 0Ω).

Testing: Ring Continuity (Stage 1)

Essential to ensure there are no open circuits (breaks) in the ring, which would cause overloading.

Phase Loop

Measure resistance end-to-end (r1)

0.3Ω

Neutral Loop

Measure resistance end-to-end (rn)

0.3Ω

Earth Loop

Measure resistance end-to-end (r2)

0.3Ω
Result: All three readings should be substantially the same (for 2.5mm² Twin & Earth, cpc is 1.5mm² so r2 will be higher, approx 1.67x r1).

Testing: Ring Continuity (Stage 2)

To confirm the absence of bridges (short cuts) in the ring.

The "Figure 8" Connection

  • Cross connect Phase 1 to Neutral 2.
  • Cross connect Phase 2 to Neutral 1.
  • Measure resistance at every socket outlet (L to N).

Expected Result

Readings at all sockets should be substantially the same.

Within ± 0.05Ω

Shaver Socket

Safety Isolating Transformer

Required for use in bathrooms (Zone 2).

  • Dual Voltage: 115V / 230V.
  • Floating: Separated from Earth potential.
  • Protection: Self-resetting thermal cut-out.
  • Switching: Insertion of plug activates supply.

Immersion Heaters

Element Construction:

Spiral Heater: Nichrome wire.
Insulation: Compacted Magnesium Oxide (MgO). Efficient heat conductor, electrical insulator.
Sheath: Copper (or Incoloy for hard water).

Thermostat

Bi-Metal Rod Type

Operates on differential expansion of Brass tube vs Invar rod.

  • Action: Snap action magnet (reduces arcing).
  • Setting: 60°C (Hard water) - 80°C (Soft water).
  • Differential: 5-15°F (2-8°C) between cut-out and cut-in.

Dual Element Configuration

SINK

Short Element

Volume: ~10 Gallons (Top of cylinder)

Power: ~2 kW

Resistance: High (~25Ω)

BATH

Long Element

Volume: ~30 Gallons (Full cylinder)

Power: ~3 kW

Resistance: Low (~17Ω)

Wiring & Control

The Circuit

  • Separate Radial Circuit.
  • 2.5mm² Twin & Earth cable.
  • Heat Resistant Flex from switch to heater.
  • Protected by 20A MCB and RCD.

The Switch

Dual Immersion Switch (Bath/Sink). Incorporates:

  • Double Pole Isolation (20A).
  • Changeover Switch (Sink/Bath).
  • Neon Indicator (On/Off).

Safety Cut-Out

To prevent boiling water if the thermostat fails closed.

  • Mechanism: Independent over-temperature cut-out.
  • Reset: Manual reset button (often on the thermostat head).
  • Operation: Will trip if water boils due to stat failure OR external heat sources (e.g. solid fuel stove).

Immersion Fault Diagnosis

Using a multimeter between Common (C) and Terminals.

Reading:

Sink to Common = 0Ω

Reading:

Bath to Common = Infinity

Reading:

Common to Stat = Infinity

Cooker Diversity Calculation

Total Load Example: 12,740 Watts (12.74 kW)

Step 1: Find Total Current

I = P / V = 12740 / 230 ≈ 55 Amps

Step 2: Apply Diversity Rule

  • First 10 Amps.
  • Plus 30% of the remainder.
  • Plus 5A if socket outlet in control unit.
Remainder = 55A - 10A = 45A
30% of 45A = 13.5A
Total = 10A + 13.5A = 23.5A

Cooker Installation

Circuit

6mm² Cable. 32A MCB protection.

Switch Location

Must be within 2 metres of the cooker, but NOT directly above it.

Connection

Via Cooker Connection Unit. Use heat resistant flex allowing cooker to be moved for cleaning.

Bathroom Zones

Zone 0

Interior of bath or shower basin.

Zone 1

Up to 2.25m height above Zone 0.

Zone 2

Extends 0.6m horizontally from Zone 1.

Zone 3

Extends 2.4m horizontally from Zone 2.

Electric Showers

High Load

Ratings 7.5kW to 10.5kW. Requires heavy cable (6mm² - 10mm²) and 40A protection.

Isolation

Ceiling mounted Pull Cord Switch.

  • Double Pole.
  • Mechanical Flag Indicator (Neon not sufficient for isolation proof).
  • Located outside Zone 0.

Shower Operation

Temperature vs Flow

Electric showers heat water instantaneously. The temperature depends on how long the water stays in contact with the element.

Fast Flow Cooler Water
Slow Flow Hotter Water

Note: In winter, incoming water is colder (5°C), so flow must be reduced to get a hot shower.

Cable Resistance Factors

Length

Resistance Increases with length.

CSA (Area)

Resistance Decreases as area increases.

Material

Copper is standard (low resistivity).

Voltage Drop Limits

ETCI Rules Max Permissible Drop:

4%

of nominal voltage

4% of 230V = 9.2 Volts

If Voltage Drop is excessive > Increase Cable Size.

Calculating Volt Drop

Formula using mV/A/m table:

VD = (mV x I x L) / 1000

Example:

  • Cable: 2.5mm² (18 mV/A/m)
  • Current (I): 15 Amps
  • Length (L): 25 Metres
VD = (18 x 15 x 25) / 1000
VD = 6750 / 1000
VD = 6.75 Volts

Power Loss in Cables

Current flowing through resistance produces heat. This is wasted energy.

Formula: P = I²R

Example Calculation:

Current (I) = 30 Amps

Resistance (R) = 0.2 Ohms

P = 30² x 0.2

P = 900 x 0.2

P = 180 Watts
