Incandescent Lamps

Principle of Operation

Current flows through a fine tungsten filament. The filament is heated to a very high temperature in an inert gas, causing it to glow.

  • Effect: Uses the Heating Effect of electric current.
  • Lifespan: Approximately 1000 Hours.

Lamp Caps

BC (Bayonet Cap)

Standard push and twist fitting. Commonly used in pendants.

ES (Edison Screw)

Screw-in fitting. Requires strict polarity.

Safety Rule: The outer screw thread must be connected to the Neutral. The centre contact is the Phase.

Discharge Lighting Issues

Stroboscopic Effect

Under discharge lighting (like older fluorescents), rotating machinery may appear stationary or moving slowly if the rotation speed matches the mains frequency (50Hz).

Danger: Moving parts may appear safe to touch.

Fluorescent Components

The Starter

Initiates the arc.

The Capacitor

A small capacitor is connected across the starter switch.

Purpose: Radio Interference Suppression.

Switching Basics

  • 1-Way Switch: Controls light from 1 position. (2 Terminals: C, L1).
  • 2-Way Switch: Controls light from 2 positions (e.g. Stairs). (3 Terminals: C, L1, L2).

The Strappers

In two-way switching, two wires connect the switches together.

Fact: Strappers are connected between the two-way switches (e.g. L1 to L1, L2 to L2).

Video: 2-Way Switching

This external video demonstrates how the current flows through the "Strappers" (L1 and L2) to control the light from two different locations.

Key Concept: The switch wire is only live when a complete path exists through the strappers.

Intermediate Switching

Used when control is required from 3 or more positions.

The Rule of N

  • Start with 2x Two-Way Switches (Ends).
  • Add Intermediate Switches in the middle for extra points.

Master Control Switch

Master ON

A switch connected in parallel with local switches.

When closed, it creates a direct path from Live to the Lamps, bypassing local switches.

Function: Over-rides local switches to turn ALL lights ON.

(Often used for security/panic lighting).

Loop-In Method

The most common domestic wiring method.

Key Point: The Live (Phase) conductor is looped at the Ceiling Rose.

A switch drop cable takes the live to the switch and returns with a "Switched Live".

Junction Box Method

Alternative to Loop-in.

  • Connections are made in a central junction box (usually in the attic/void).
  • Only the flex goes to the light fitting.
  • Requirement: Junction boxes must remain accessible for inspection.

Flexible Cord Sizes

Application

Used from the Ceiling Rose to the Lampholder (Pendant).

Minimum Size

According to ETCI rules:

0.75 mm²

(Note: 0.5mm was used historically, but 0.75mm is the robust standard).

Counting Conductors

Scenario: Two fluorescent fittings in PVC conduit controlled by one switch. How many wires at the link (Point X)?

The Breakdown:

  1. Phase (Live): Feed looping to next point.
  2. Neutral: Return path.
  3. Earth: Protection.
  4. Switched Phase: From switch to lamp.

Total = 4 Conductors

Counting Conductors is vital for Conduit Capacity (Space Factor).

Switch Mounting Heights

Accessible Zone

Switches should be mounted between:

900mm - 1200mm

From finished floor level.

Circuit Quantities

  • Min Circuits: A domestic installation must have at least 2 lighting circuits.
  • Max Points: Maximum 10 points (lights) per circuit.

Wattage Cap

Total load should not exceed 1200W per circuit (approx).

This aligns with the 10 point rule (assuming 100W per point).

Protection (MCB)

Sizing the MCB

For a standard lighting circuit wired in 1.5mm² copper:

10 Amps

(6A is also common, but 10A is standard for 1.5mm).

Fuse Rating Principle

The current rating of a fuse/MCB is determined by:

The capacity of the smallest conductor in the circuit.

Protection protects the CABLE, not the appliance.

Supply Equipment Sequence

1. Service Fuse
2. Meter
3. Main Switch
4. Protective Device
Correct Order: Fuse, Meter, Switch, Protection.

Polarity Testing

What is it?

Verifying that the Phase (Live) conductor is connected to the correct points.

Why do it?

  • To ensure the Switch is on the Live wire.
  • To ensure the Fuse/MCB is on the Live wire.
  • To ensure ES lamp centre pin is Live.
