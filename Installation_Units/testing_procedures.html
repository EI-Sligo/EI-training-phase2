Introduction & Certification

New installations must be inspected and tested to ensure compliance with NSAI National Rules.

Tests must be carried out by competent persons in a specific sequence.

Certification

The CRU (Commission for Regulation of Utilities) oversees the process. Safe Electric issues certificates.

Phase 2 Scope

Only Pre-connection tests will be carried out in Phase 2.

Visual Inspection

Must be done before testing and before making live.

Checklist

  • Correct cable type and current rating.
  • Conductors identified correctly (Brown/Blue).
  • No damage to cables/enclosures.
  • Terminations tight and mechanically sound.
  • Fire barriers in trunking.
  • Bonding complete.

The Sequence of Testing

1. DEAD TESTS (No Supply)

  1. Continuity of Conductors.
  2. Insulation Resistance.
  3. Erroneous Connections.
  4. Polarity Test (Dead Method).

2. LIVE TESTS (Supply On)

  1. Fault Loop Impedance (Zs).
  2. RCD Operation.
  3. Functional Tests.

Test Instruments

Low Reading Ohmmeter

For Continuity and Polarity. Must deliver > 0.2 Amps.

Insulation Resistance Tester

Uses 500V DC to stress insulation.

Pre-Use Checks

  • Check Battery & Leads.
  • Null/Zero the leads.

Continuity: Rp + Re Method

Verifies integrity of Protective Conductors (CPC).

Procedure

  1. Connect Phase to Earth at the DB.
  2. Zero test leads.
  3. Measure resistance at each outlet.
  4. Record the highest reading.

Continuity: Re Method

Uses a long wander lead. Ideal for bonding conductors.

Procedure

  1. Null wander lead.
  2. Connect to MET at board.
  3. Probe outlet Earth.
  4. Record highest reading.

Bonding Requirements

Main Bonding

Connects Gas, Water, Oil to MET.

Note: Disconnect one end first to ensure no parallel paths.

Supplementary Bonding

  • Bathroom: Radiators, pipes, metal baths.
  • Kitchen: Sinks, hot/cold pipes.

Ring Circuit Step 1

Verifies end-to-end continuity.

Procedure

  • Separate all conductors at DB.
  • Measure L-L, N-N, and E-E loops.
  • Difference should be no more than 0.05Ω.

Insulation Resistance (6.4.3.3)

Purpose & Prep

  • Disconnect electronic appliances.
  • Close all switches.
  • Disconnect Surge Protection Devices (SPDs).

P & N to Earth

Procedure

  1. Link Phase and Neutral together at DB.
  2. Switch ON all MCBs.
  3. Test between Earth terminal and the P/N link.
  4. Voltage: 500V DC.

Phase to Neutral

Warning

Ensure ALL loads are removed. High voltage damages equipment.

Procedure

  • Set tester to 500V.
  • Switch on MCBs.
  • Test between Phase busbar and Neutral terminal.

Erroneous Connections

The Test

  1. Disconnect all loads.
  2. Switch OFF the MCB of the circuit you are testing.
  3. Switch ON all other MCBs.
  4. Measure IR (500V) between Phase of disconnected circuit and Busbar.

Result: A low reading indicates a cross-connection.

Polarity Test (6.4.3.6)

Ensures Phase is connected to fuses/switches and ES centre contacts.

Procedure

  • Link Phase and Earth at DB.
  • Check outlet Phase to Earth: Low Resistance.
  • Check outlet Neutral to Earth: Infinity.

Live Testing

Hazards

  • Contact with live parts.
  • Short circuits by probes.
  • Faulty leads.

Tests

  • Fault Loop Impedance.
  • RCD Verification.
  • Phase Sequence.

Fault Loop Impedance (Zs)

Ensures fault current is high enough to trip MCB.

(Rp + Re) + Ze = Zs

Tested at the furthest outlet of each circuit.
