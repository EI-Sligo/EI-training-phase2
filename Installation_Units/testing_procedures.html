Introduction & Regulation

New installations and extensions must be inspected and tested to ensure compliance with NSAI National Rules for Electrical Installations.

Tests must be carried out by competent persons using prescribed equipment in a specific sequence.

Certification

Certificates are now online. The CRU (Commission for Regulation of Utilities) oversees the process, and Safe Electric issues certificates.

Phase 2 Scope

Please note: Only Pre-connection tests will be carried out in Phase 2.

Sequence of Tests (Chapter 6)

  • Voltage drop (6.4.3.11)

Continuity of Conductors

Must be verified for: Protective conductors (including bonding), exposed conductive parts, and ring final circuit conductors.

Method 1: Rp + Re

  1. Connect Phase to Earth at Distribution Board.
  2. Zero test leads.
  3. Measure resistance between Phase and Earth at each outlet.
  4. Record the highest reading.

Continuity: Re Method

Uses a long wander lead.

Procedure

  1. Null/Zero the resistance of the wander lead and test leads.
  2. Connect one end of wander lead to Main Earth Terminal (MET).
  3. Connect other end to test probe.
  4. Take reading at the Earth of each outlet.
  5. Record highest value.

Continuity of Bonding

Verified via the Re method.

What to Test:

  • Main Equipotential Bonding: Disconnect one end first to ensure no parallel paths.
  • Bathroom Bonding: Radiators, pipework.
  • Kitchen Equipment: Sinks, food prep areas.

Ring Circuit Continuity

Verifies the ring is complete with no loose connections.

Procedure

  • Separate all conductors at DB.
  • Null test leads.
  • Measure resistance end-to-end of:
    • Live (L-L)
    • Neutral (N-N)
    • Earth (E-E)

Difference should be no more than 0.01Ω.

Insulation Resistance

Purpose & Prep

Ensures no short circuits or insulation damage.

  • Disconnect electronic appliances (Smoke alarms, LEDs, computers) to prevent damage.
  • Close all switches (check 2-ways).
  • Disconnect Surge Protection Devices (SPDs).

Phase & Neutral to Earth

Procedure

  1. Link Phase and Neutral together at DB.
  2. Switch ON all MCBs (Tests complete installation).
  3. Test between Earth terminal and the P/N link.
  4. Voltage: 500V DC.

If result is low, isolate circuits individually to find the fault.

Phase to Neutral

Critical Warning

Ensure ALL loads (lamps, appliances) are removed. High voltage can damage equipment.

Procedure

  • Set tester to 500V (250V pre-test recommended).
  • Switch on MCBs.
  • Test between Phase busbar and Neutral terminal.

Erroneous Connections (6.4.3.3.2)

The Test

  1. Disconnect all loads.
  2. Switch OFF the MCB of the circuit you are testing.
  3. Switch ON all other MCBs.
  4. Measure Insulation Resistance (500V) between the Phase of the disconnected circuit and the Busbar (all other phases).

Result: A low reading indicates an erroneous connection (cross-connection) between circuits.

Polarity Test

Ensures Phase is connected to fuses/switches and ES centre contacts.

Step 1: Phase-Earth Link

  • Link Phase and Earth at DB.
  • Check outlet Phase to Earth: Low Resistance.
  • Check outlet Neutral to Earth: Infinity.

Polarity: N-E Check

Step 2: Neutral-Earth Link

Repeat the procedure with a link between Neutral and Earth at the DB.

This verifies the Neutral is connected and that Phase/Earth are not reversed.

Note: Polarity can often be verified during the Rp+Re continuity test.

Post-Connection (Live) Tests

These tests are performed after the installation is energised.

Automatic Disconnection (6.4.3.7)

  • Earth Fault Loop Impedance (6.4.3.7.3).
  • Verification of RCD operation.

Equipment

Multifunction Testers (MFT) are typically used.

Earth Fault Loop Impedance

Determines if sufficient fault current will flow to trip the MCB/RCBO.

(Rp + Re) + Ze = Zs
  • Ze: External Impedance (Supply).
  • Zs: Total Impedance (Measured at furthest outlet).

Example Calculation (32A Type B)

Type B trips at 5x rated current.

Trip Current = 32A x 5 = 160A.

Resistance = 230V / 160A = 1.4375Ω (Rounded to 1.44Ω).

Temp Correction

Resistance increases with temperature. Measured Zs must be corrected.

Zs(m) ≤ 2/3 x (Uo / Ia)

Recommended limit is 2/3 of table value.

Disconnection Times

  • ≤ 32A: 0.4 Seconds (Final Circuits).
  • > 32A: 5 Seconds (Distribution).
